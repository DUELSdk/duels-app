# DUEL Direction

The north star. Read this before making any product, design, copy, or build decision. When something feels off, check it against this first.

---

## Why It Exists

Competitive players want money on the line with no luck element. The options today are casinos (luck-based, licensed, restricted) or free play (no stakes). DUEL fills the exact gap in between: real money, 100% skill, no gambling license required.

That gap is the whole business. Every decision should protect or expand it.

---

## What It Is

A competition platform. Players duel for a purse. The platform hosts, enforces, and takes a fee. It never participates in outcomes. It is a tournament organizer, not a casino.

The duel is the product. Everything else — themes, cosmetics, tournaments, the ticket system — serves that core transaction.

---

## The Feel

> "A library of confrontations."

Dark. Restrained. Intentional. The aesthetic communicates that something real is at stake — not through noise, but through the absence of it.

- Underdo everything. A split line, a mirrored element, a moment of tension. If you have to point it out, it's too much.
- Stakes over features. Describe what you're playing for, not how the platform works.
- Short, aggressive, direct copy. No exclamation marks except win states.

Not: pitch black gamer aesthetic, neon, overwrought, decorated.
Yes: dark charcoal, red as tension not decoration, structure that feels earned.

---

## The Core Mechanic

Every game on DUEL uses **simultaneous decisions**. Both players commit before either sees. Both think at the same time, lock in, reveal together.

This is non-negotiable and defines the platform.

It does three things:
1. Eliminates reaction advantage — no turn order, no waiting
2. Breaks memorized theory — you can't react to a move you never see
3. Adds a prediction layer on top of strategy — reading your opponent matters as much as knowing the game

Any game that doesn't have this doesn't belong on DUEL.

---

## What DUEL Must Never Become

- A casino. No RNG. No luck element. Ever.
- A traditional gaming platform. This isn't about fun — it's about competition and money on the line.
- Loud or decorated. Neon, overwrought UI, "gamer" aesthetic — all wrong.
- Pay-to-win. No real money cosmetics. Tickets only, cosmetics only.
- Complicated to enter. The confirmation screen is non-negotiable. Fee is never a surprise.

---

## Legal Guardrails

Danish gambling law requires all three: entry fee + prize + **chance element**. DUEL eliminates the third. This is the legal foundation and it must be protected at every layer.

- Every game must be 100% skill, 0% RNG
- Platform framing: competition platform, tournament organizer
- Copy framing: never "bet", "wager", "indsats", "jackpot", "odds", "house edge"
- Business model framing: entry fee (not rake) — platform charges for a service, never touches the prize pot

## Strategic Direction

**Now:** Bootstrap. Copenhagen. Prove the concept locally.
**Next:** National Denmark → Germany → Sweden → France/Spain.
**Later:** EU-wide. Raise after first real traction.

UK deprioritized (post-Brexit complexity). Norway, Finland, Netherlands, Belgium, Italy, Japan, UAE/MENA, USA blocked day one.

Launch ad strategy: one OOH hero in Copenhagen (statement, not campaign) + TikTok/Instagram with live pot counter and player count. The social content does the long game.

---

## What a Good Decision Looks Like

A good decision makes the duel sharper, the legal position cleaner, or the player experience more direct. It does not add noise, complexity, or distance between the player and the outcome.

When in doubt: does this make the confrontation feel more real, or less?
